Research

Overview

My research examines the adaptation of autonomous behaviours for entities in participatory virtual environments. To address this, I focus on three behavioural properties when modelling virtual entities: the entity must learn by doing; the entity is equipped with an autonomous simulated world to anticipate the behaviour of "its" world; and the entity treats human participants as privileged actors to guide the adaptation of its behaviour — human "in the loop". Each application below addresses one or more of these three points.

Applications

SAGITTAIRE Robocup@home · sociocommunicative gesture

Sociocommunicative Abilities by Gesture Improvement Through Temporal Active Interaction in Robotic Environments. For credible human–robot interaction, robots need non-verbal behaviour similar to a human's, but human interaction is too complex to hand-code as behaviour patterns. In SAGITTAIRE, built for RoboCup@home, unsupervised neural networks learn from large video datasets to model perception, decision and action for a robotic agent: perceiving human behaviour, choosing an appropriate response, and generating the corresponding action.

Supervision: A. Legeleux (Master Thesis)

INCIDENT plant disease identification

INtelligent Coffee gradIng anD disEase ideNtificaTion — a system that detects and classifies plant diseases from leaf photographs taken in uncontrolled, real-world conditions, using a convolutional neural network to reach high classification accuracy on images taken "in the wild".

Supervision: S. Wallelign (PhD) · Collaboration: Jimma Institute of Technology, Ethiopia

  • S. Wallelign, M. Polceanu, C. Buche — Soybean Plant Disease Identification Using Convolutional Neural Network, FLAIRS, AAAI Press, 2018.
SOMBRERO ANR · humanoid teleoperation

An ANR project training humanoid robots (Nina and Roméo) with sociocommunicative abilities through immersive teleoperation — "beaming" of human pilots into the robot.

Supervision: M. Polceanu (post-doc) · Collaboration: GIPSA-Lab, LIG, LIP, Aldebaran Robotics

  • F. Lasson, M. Polceanu, C. Buche, P. De Loor — Temporal Deep Belief Network for Online Human Action Recognition, FLAIRS, pages 80–85, AAAI Press, 2017.
Smart Tennis Court low-cost video tracking

A modular system that tracks players and ball-rebound locations during a tennis match in real time from a single, fixed, medium-resolution camera — designed to run on low-cost equipment such as a Raspberry Pi camera, to derive game statistics from ordinary video.

Collaboration: Ericsson · M2S lab · Granit lab (IRISA)

  • M. Polceanu, A. Petac, H. Ben Lebsir, B. Fiter, C. Buche — Real Time Tennis Match Tracking With Low Cost Equipment, FLAIRS, AAAI Press, 2018.
MIHO bot believability evaluation

Believable, human-like opponents are a real benefit to a game's entertainment value, but evaluation methods vary widely, making systems hard to compare. Building on the BotPrize competition, MIHO partly automates such believability evaluations and analyses judge behaviour from a national event: the most reliable judges turned out to be frequent players of combat-oriented games, used to playing against virtual, strange, and physically present opponents.

Supervision: C. Even (PhD) · Collaboration: Virtualys, GeNeura Lab (Spain)

  • C. Even, A-G. Bosser, C. Buche — Analysis of the Protocols Used to Assess Virtual Players in Multi-player Computer Games, IWANN, pages 657–668, LNCS, 2017.
REVAM out-of-body VR for anorexia

Uses virtual reality to induce an out-of-body experience in the context of body perception, contributing to a clinical study that helps people with eating disorders improve their body image.

Supervision: G. Biannic (Master Thesis) · Collaboration: Bohars Psychiatry Hospital (France)

  • C. Buche, N. Le Bigot — REVAM: a virtual reality application for inducing body size perception modifications, International Conference on Cyberworlds, pages 229–236, IEEE, 2018.
HYPERION on-robot object detection

Tests and optimises machine-learning and vision solutions for object detection on NAO robots, so a robot can tell teammates from opponents and localise them. The constraint: detection must run under 5% CPU and within 20ms per cycle on the robot itself.

Supervision: A. Petac (Master Thesis) · Collaboration: University of Miami (USA)

  • M. Polceanu, F. Harrouet, C. Buche — Fast Multi-Scale fHOG Feature Extraction Using Histogram Downsampling, RoboCup Symposium, 2018.
IVT interactive virtual training for teachers

Interactive Virtual Training places early-career teachers in a 3D virtual classroom populated by interactive virtual students simulating disruptive behaviour, giving novice teachers a safe space to practise classroom management before they start teaching. Two tailored scenarios simulate classrooms from under-resourced neighbourhoods, one for 1st–2nd grade and one for 6th–7th grade.

Supervision: A. Delamarre (PhD Thesis) · Collaboration: Florida International University (Affective Social Computing Lab), Rutgers University (Psychology), Virginia Tech (Industrial & Systems Engineering)

  • A. Delamarre, C. Buche, S. Lunn, G. Ruiz, E. Shernoff, C. Lisetti — Interactive Virtual Training (IVT), a serious game to help early career teachers with classroom management, FLAIRS, pages 400–403, AAAI Press, 2017.
REHABILITATION emotional conversational agent

Combines an emotional conversational agent with an interactive-storytelling narrative engine to generate varied rehabilitation situations, blending role-play and problem-solving exercises to support a patient's progress and motivation.

Supervision: C. Even (Master Thesis) · Collaboration: Florida International University, Teesside University, Bohars Psychiatry Hospital

  • C. Even, A-G. Bosser, J.F. Ferreira, C. Buche, F. Stephan, M. Cavazza, C. Lisetti — Supporting Social Skills Rehabilitation with Virtual Storytelling, FLAIRS, pages 329–334, AAAI Press, 2016.
PREDICT clinical decision support

A machine-learning-based clinical decision support tool that detects the type and severity of haemophilia from thrombograms — temporal series describing how the thrombin rate evolves during a haemorrhage. Funded by Diagnostica Stago.

Supervision: A. Delamarre (Master Thesis), F. Lasson (PhD Thesis)

CHAMELEON imitation learning in video games

Built with Virtualys, CHAMELEON acquires behaviour by imitating humans inside video-game environments: a player takes control of an entity by substitution, and the entity learns behaviours better adapted to its environment directly from the human, rather than from a score-based criterion. What matters is believability — approaching "human standards" — rather than performance. Video games give researchers a ready testbed (rendering, physics and networking already solved) and a community of expert human players able to judge believability.

Supervision: F. Tence (Master & PhD Thesis), J. Soler (PhD Thesis)

  • J. Soler, F. Tence, L. Gaubert, C. Buche — Data Clustering and Similarity, FLAIRS, pages 492–495, AAAI, 2013.
  • F. Tence, L. Gaubert, J. Soler, P. De Loor, C. Buche — CHAMELEON: Online Learning for Believable Behaviors based on Humans Imitation in Computer Games, CAVW, 24(5):477–495, 2013.
  • F. Tence, L. Gaubert, J. Soler, P. De Loor, C. Buche — Stable Growing Neural Gas: a Topology Learning Algorithm based on Player Tracking in Video Games, Applied Soft Computing, 13(10):4174–4184, 2013.
ORPHEUS mental simulation framework

Inspired by how humans perform mental simulation to anticipate events, ORPHEUS is a generic agent architecture that makes decisions by simulating functional models of the world ahead of time — imagining outcomes before acting, rather than relying on a task-specific simulator.

Supervision: M. Polceanu (PhD Thesis)

  • M. Polceanu, C. Buche — Computational mental simulation: a review, CAVW, 2016.
  • C. Buche, N. Le Bigot, M. Polceanu — Simulation within Simulation for Agent Decision-Making: Theoretical Foundations from Cognitive Science to Operational Computer Model, Cognitive Systems Research, 40:46–58, 2016.
JABU anticipatory virtual juggler

An artificial 3D juggler that predicts the trajectory of balls in the air and uses that prediction to coordinate its own movement — a proactive agent driven by internal simulation, with an interface that lets a human throw a ball to it directly.

Supervision: A. Jeannin-Girardon (Master Thesis)

  • C. Buche, P. De Loor — Anticipatory behavior in virtual universe, application to a virtual juggler, CAVW, 24(2):111–125, 2013.
CAMUS fuzzy cognitive maps

Models emotional, perceptual behaviour with fuzzy cognitive maps, where internal states are explicitly represented and drive an entity's response to external stimuli. Demonstrated through a shepherd, his herd, and sheepdogs, where any entity can be substituted for direct human control.

Collaboration: Virtual and Augmented Reality Research Laboratory (Romania)

  • C. Buche, P. Chevaillier, A. Nédélec, M. Parenthoën, J. Tisseau — Fuzzy cognitive maps for the simulation of individual adaptive behaviors, CAVW, 21(6):573–587, 2010.
GEMEAU classifier systems

A generic model for online learning through classifier systems — flexible enough to interface with different environments, test many classifier variants and compare them fairly. Applied to labyrinth-style tasks and schedule-management software, and later reused in the PEGASE project.

Supervision: E. Creac'h (Master Thesis)

  • C. Buche, P. De Loor — Generic model for experimenting and using a family of classifiers systems, ICAISC, LNCS/LNAI 6113, pages 299–306, Springer, 2010.
PEGASE generic intelligent tutoring system

An adaptive, generic intelligent tutoring system (ITS) embedded in a virtual training environment, providing pedagogical assistance to both learner and teacher. A multi-agent system analyses the learner's actions to build a "pedagogical situation" and assist the trainer's decisions; the pedagogical agent's behaviour is itself a hierarchical classifier system that adapts through reinforcement from the trainer. Funded by Diagnostica Stago.

Supervision: G. Faudet, T.H. Trinh, Y. Cardin (Master), F. Le Corre, Y. Cardin (PhD)

  • C. Buche, R. Querrec — An expert system manipulating knowledge to help human learners into virtual environment, Expert Systems With Applications, 38(7):8446–8457, 2011.
  • C. Buche, C. Bossard, R. Querrec, P. Chevaillier — PEGASE: A generic and adaptable intelligent system for virtual reality learning environments, IJVR, 9(2):73–85, 2010.
